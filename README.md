<p style="text-align: justify;">
Effective population visualizations are essential for communicating future resource demands. This project examines an existing bar chart based on World Bank data that compares Asian population figures in 1995 and projected values for 2025. The original visualization relies on three-dimensional bars and inconsistent ordering, which hinders accurate comparison and obscures key growth patterns.

<p style="text-align: justify;">
We redesign the chart using established information visualization principles with a strong emphasis on accessibility and interpretability. A two-dimensional layout with consistent axes and clearer labeling replaces the original 3D design, while a color-blind–friendly palette is adopted to ensure readability for a wider range of users. In addition, an interactive geographic map of Asia is introduced to provide spatial context, along with interactive features such as tooltips and dynamic highlighting. Together, these design choices improve visual clarity, support precise comparison, and enable more inclusive exploration of population growth patterns and their implications for future food demand.
